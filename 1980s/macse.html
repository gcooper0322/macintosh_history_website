The Macintosh SE was introduced in March 1987 as a more modular and expandable computer in the same form factor as the original Macintosh ("SE" standing for "System Expansion").
It contained a number of new features and technologies, including a proprietary internal expansion slot, an internal drive bay that could take either a second floppy drive or an internal hard drive, Apple Desktop Bus (ADB) for keyboard and mouse connections, and an improved SCSI bus that could achieve double the throughput of the Macintosh Plus' SCSI bus. It used the same CPU as the previous all-in-one models (an 8MHz Motorola 68000), and shipped with the same RAM (1MB expandable to 4MB) and floppy drive as the Macintosh Plus. It sold for $2,900 in the dual-floppy-drive configuration, or $3,900 with one floppy drive and a 20MB internal hard drive.

An enhanced model known as the SE/30 was introduced in January 1989. It was mostly the same as the regular SE, but with upgrades including a 16MHz 68030 CPU (hence the "30" in the name), 4MB of RAM expandable to 128MB, a 1.44MB floppy drive, and a 40MB or 80MB internal hard drive. It sold for $4,369.
