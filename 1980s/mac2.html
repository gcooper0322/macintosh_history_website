The Macintosh II, introduced in March 1987, was the first Macintosh model to support color graphics, and the first not to have a built-in monitor, instead having a horizontal desktop case with a separate monitor.
It shipped with a 16MHz Motorola 68020 CPU, 1MB of RAM (expandable to 8MB), one or two internal floppy drives, an optional 40MB or 80MB internal hard drive, and a 12-inch monochrome or 13-inch color monitor, selling for between $5,500 and $7,200.
The last variant of the Macintosh II was discontinued in October 1993.

The Macintosh IIx was the same as the regular II, but used a 16MHz Motorola 68030 CPU, added a 4MB RAM configuration option, increased the maximum RAM to a theoretical 128MB, and was one of the two first Macintosh models to include a 1.44MB floppy drive as standard. Its initial price was $7,769 for a basic configuration.

The Macintosh IIcx and IIci were introduced in March and September 1989, respectively. The main difference from earlier models was a case that was 7 inches narrower (hence the "c" for "compact"). This meant that it lacked the space for a second floppy drive, but by that point, a second floppy drive was rapidly becoming unnecessary due to higher RAM and disk capacities. The IIci was simply a more powerful version of the IIcx, with a 25MHz 68030 CPU replacing the 16MHz one.
The IIcx initially sold for $5,369, and the IIci initially sold for $6,269.

The Macintosh IIfx was the most powerful member of the Macintosh II family upon its introduction in March 1990, shipping with a 40MHz 68030 CPU and 4MB of RAM as standard. Options included an 80MB or 160MB internal hard drive.
All that speed and power came at a high price: between $9,000 and $12,000.

The Macintosh IIsi was introduced in October 1990 as a lower-cost alternative to other Macintosh II variants. It shipped with a 20MHz 68030 CPU, 1MB of RAM, a single 1.44MB floppy drive, and a 40MB or 80MB hard drive at an introductory price of $2,999.

The Macintosh IIvi and IIvx were both introduced in October 1992. The main difference between the two was in processor speed - the IIvi had a 16MHz 68030 CPU, while the IIvx had a 32MHz one. These were the only Macintosh II variants that could be equipped with an internal CD-ROM drive. They both sold for $3,000.
