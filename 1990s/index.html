The early and mid-1990s: when the Macintosh line expanded... to an extreme.
Apple very nearly went bankrupt in 1997 because they had introduced so many models with negligible differences that their customers had become confused and sales had plummeted.
Also during this time, the Macintosh switched to a different CPU architecture for the first time: from Motorola 68000 to PowerPC.
